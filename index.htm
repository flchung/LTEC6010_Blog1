Eurat's article: Educational technology: conceptual frameworks and historical development

Posted on September 1, 2019 by Fu-Ling Chung

 

This article shows us a simple history of educational technology, which helps me understand the concept of educational technology more. To the best of my knowledge, realizing the history of one subject is significant for people before learning it. However, the author does not provide detailed information about many scholars’ theories that are mentioned or explaining the terminologies in the educational technology field. Besides, the article is not like a standard research paper because there is no literature review and conclusion part of the article.

 

To begin with, it is difficult for readers to find a core topic to follow. The author mentions many different ideas without explaining and developing. The concept changes by the timeline and it is not clear to know everything deeply. There are so many people, countries, subjects, organizations, and years that makes me confused in the article. The author should provide more information instead of leaving lots of space and time for readers to imagine by themselves. To make the article more reader-friendly, the author could narrow every section down and avoid present bountiful unimportant information.

 

Also, the organization of the article is not wonderful. If someone wants to arrange part of one history, he or she should consider more carefully before writing. The article seems to me that the author wants to include everything about educational technology in this article, but he or she did not deal with the feelings of readers. No one is willing to spend time reading something long but get something barely essential from it. Without explaining the terminologies, most people cannot fully comprehend what the article display. Most of the time the author just claims some scholars and organizations give a new theory or definition, and we cannot know what else the author prefers to state.

 

Finally, the author does not follow professional rules to write this article. Missing citations, explanations of terminologies, and conclusion are the clues to demonstrate the article is not a standard research paper. It is not easy for people to get the key point immediately. Readers have to go through every part of the article and maybe they can find something critical. In my case, I cannot understand what the author wants to say when I finish the article. The more I read, the more I felt confused. As a result, I disagree that this article is a good example to write a history of educational technology.
